What does it mean?

The heatmap, on the left, allows to visualize the correlation and, thus, the link between two attributes. The attributes represented in this visualization are the numerical attributes. Here we have 9 attributes which are numeric: year_of_release, user_count, critic_count, critic_score, user_score, NA_Sales, EU_Sales, JP_Sales, Other_Sales. Hovering over a box would display a tooltip which shows the correlation score, located between -1 and 1. A score of 1 means that the attributes are perfectly correlated and a score of -1 would be the case where the attributes are perfectly negatively correlated . A score of 0 between two attributes would therefore mean that there is no correlation between the two attributes. The best example for a score of 1 would be when we look at the correlation of an attribute with itself (the diagonal).
Clicking on any heatmap box would display a scatter plot in the top tile. This scatter plot would have as axis the two desired attributes. The more the points of the graph are in the form of a line, the more they are correlated. By default, the correlation graph is between the attributes year_of_release and critic_count.
